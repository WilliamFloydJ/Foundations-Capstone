Pre-War

The Great Depression ravaged the whole world from 1929 to 1939. 10 years of poverty and pain for the vast majority of the populous. Brought on by the end of the First World War, or the Great War as it was known, economically and emotionally it was truly a depression. A generation trying to find itself , the United States lugged through the failed presidency of Herbert Hoover then president Franklin D. Roosevelt took the nation by storm. As the president with the longest time in office, Roosevelt took the nation from one of its darkest points to eventually being known as the Greatest Generation in U.S. History. Putting into effect the New Deal as he coined it, effectively sparked a fire underneath the feet of the American people and gave hope to millions nationwide.

The Beginning

The second world war started officially on September 1st 1939, with the invasion of Poland by the Nazi’s. However the United States didn’t join the war till December 7th 1941, with the attack on the naval port Pearl Harbor by the Imperial Japanese. The date which will live in infamy, gave entry for the United States to bolster its industry to incredible feats. Essentially turning the Great Depression on its head, by turning The United States into an economic giant. Rebuilding its navy after the attack on Pearl Harbor was no small feat, however the rebuild gave the US the chance for renovation and improvements across the board.They saw the importance of Aircraft Carriers over the previous enforced battleships. The Philippines and small islands across the Pacific were Japan’s focus in the earlier years. The Japanese invaded the Philippines 10 hours after Pearl Harbor. Then took Wake Island shortly after. As the tide might have been in Japan’s favor, The US successfully air raided Tokyo and surrounding islands during the Doolittle Raids.

The Tides Change

By May of 1942, the Japanese forces were winning on all fronts. However the battle of the coral sea revealed the weaknesses in their main naval fleets. As the first naval battle in history to have been fought with aircraft carriers, the U.S. successfully damaged two of their flag aircraft carriers. A battle with no clear victory as the United States strategically won on the fact that the Japanese were prevented from invading the port of Moresby in Papua New Guinea , and the obvious damage done to the imperial fleet. However the Japanese were able to take over the British controlled Solomon Islands and wet a larger foothold over the Pacific. Shortly after in June of the same year, one of the most important naval battles in world history took place, the famed Battle of Midway. The Japanese plan to subvert the US forces away from Midway island and take over Midway, Hawaii, Fiji, and the Samoan Islands came to halt on the 3rd of June. The United States decrypted one of the Japanese radio signals and saw through their facade. Instead, they sent the major part of their fleet to meet the Japanese at Midway, resulting in one of the most spectacular events in the war so far. Four of the Japanese aircraft carriers were completely destroyed leaving the rest of the Japanese to retreat and the Americans to go on a 3 year offensive.

The War In Europe

As the Americans continuously swept through the pacific taking island by island. On the other side of the globe, Nazi Germany has successfully swept through the major parts of Europe, Africa and is pushing slowly into the cold reaches of the Soviet Union. In November of 1942 The United States and its allies invaded northern Africa through Operation Torch. Ultimately resulting in the complete allied control of French west africa. Through these assaults the allies pushed through Axis controlled Tunisia and Sicily eventually hitting mainland Italy ruled by the fascist Mussolini. By this time in 1943 the soviets had finally been able to push back the Germans and have them on the run. Then in mid 1944 the Allies successfully invaded German occupied France in the battle of Normandy also known as D-Day. This was the largest amphibious assault in History. Pushing through France liberating city by city, eventually the Americans reached the arden forest in Luxembourg where the Germans went on their last grand offensive. The allies were inflicted with massive casualties however in this offensive, later known as the battle of the bulge, crippled the German forces allowing the Americans to take over the remainder of German territory. By April of 1945 the Soviets had marched all the way to the German capital of Berlin, marking the End of Nazi Germany and its rain over Europe. However even with the odds stacked against them Imperial Japan sought to stay in power.

The End in The Pacific

In the Pacific the Americans fought through fierce defenses by the Imperial Japanese as they fought through the Pacific. By 1944 the Americans fought in the largest carrier battle in history through the battle of the Philippine Sea. This destroyed the Japanese assault capability with their aircraft carriers and allowed the Americans to start their offensive through the Philippines. Then in February of 1945 the Americans assaulted Iwo Jima which ultimately became the most fierce Japanese defensive and was without a doubt the most intense battle in the Pacific Theater. This came to a cross in April of 1945 with the assault on the island of Okinawa, the last stand of the Japanese forces. This was the largest assault and ultimately came with the highest casualties for both sides in the Pacific theater. The battle of Okinawa was ultimately the last ground battle against the Japanese. As the Americans tried time and again to have the emperor Hirohito surrender the war, this was of no luck. The scientist however, in what is known as the Manhattan project came to produce the first working Atomic Bomb in July of that year. Generals . President Roosevelt allowed the first atomic bomb to be dropped on foreign soil in human history. This took place on August 6th, 1945 in the quaint city of Hiroshima. However even though this obliterated the city, the Emperor still refused to surrender. This prompted the United States to drop a second atomic bomb, this time on the city of Nagasaki. Also completely destroying the city and almost all of its inhabitants. These have been the only nuclear bombs to have ever been used in an armed conflict. The nuclear bombs coupled with the massive invasion of imperial controlled Manchuria by the Soviets, led to the eventual surrender by the Emperor and end to the largest and deadliest war to have ever been fought on face of the Earth.
