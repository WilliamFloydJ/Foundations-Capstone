France

The Fall of France

In the 1920’s France started construction on what was known as the Maginot Line. A series of forts along the French and German Border. This was designed to prevent Germany from ever being able to invade France again. Through the years leading up to World war 2 France was led by Albert Lebrun and his second in command Paul Reynaud. Being in close ties with the United Kingdom they were known as the Allies. In 1939 with the invasion of Poland by the Germans, the Allies declared war on Nazi Germany. This caused the Allies to put thousands of troops along the Belgium and German Border. This was because they suspected Germany would do what they did in World War One and invade Belgium then through Belgium invade France, in turn avoiding the forts along the French German border. With the construction of the Maginot Line complete this was without a doubt their plan. Through this time the concept of a tank was also developing rapidly. Even though France was the first country to implement a tank in World War 1, they only saw them as temporary weapons and would no longer be needed in modern warfare. Germany however saw tanks in the complete opposite way. Germany’s Panzer tanks were agile, powerful, and durable, unlike the french equivalent which was slow and weak. In this way the Allied forces maneuvered to only concentrate forces along the open stretches where they thought the Germans could easily send tanks and large amounts of troops. In doing so left open a large area known as the Ardennes, a large forest in Belgium and Luxembourg. From 1939 to mid 1940 not much happened in the war and it was coined the Phoney war. Then on the 10th of May 1940 Germany Invaded France through the hole in the Ardennes where France left wide open. Flooding the forest with tanks and men they advanced around the allies forces and pushed them all the way back to the sea. The French government surrendered on the 22nd of June 1940. Then Germany installed what was called Vichy France, a semi independent state that controlled the southern part of France whereas the north was controlled by the Germans directly. Vichy France like was ruled by Philippe Pétain, once a first world war hero, now considered a Nazi sympathizer and hated by most french people. However at this time a large resistance movement had started, and growing over the years.

The Liberation of France

Vichy France controlled not only the southern half of France but the colonial holdings in northern Africa and the islands in the mediterranean. In 1942 Attempts were made by the Vichy government to take a larger stance in the war effort. While Germany was hung up with the Soviets in the east, They saw it as a decent idea to take into larger security of western Europe. However right after Operation Torch in November 1942 which was a colossal invasion by the Allies now comprising the United States, they invaded Northern France. Fearing Vichy would not hold its borders, Germany took control over all of France and its territories. This effectively left Vichy France helpless and out of conflict until it’s Reluctant demise in september of 1944. By 1943 the resistance movements had taken full swing requiring more and more German SS officers to control the populace. Bombings and small arm militia rein terror on the Germans controlling France. Charles de Gaulle once a army commander, now stayed in England presiding over the efforts to retake France and rid it of the Nazi scum. On June 6th of 1944 the Allies accumulated the largest landing force ever comprised in human history, and invaded the beaches of Normandy, France. As the starting point of the reclamation of France. Leading up to the Normandy invasion the allies enacted Operation Bodyguard, a code name for a force of men whose goal was to deceive the Germans away from the actual landing point in Normandy. On the day of the invasion the American landing sites were codenamed Utah and Omaha , The British had landing sites, Sword and Gold, and finally the Canadians had landing site Juno. The initial day of the invasion, June 6th, was ultimately a failure as they did not meet their objectives. This was eventually known as D-day, purely a codename, though over 10,000 men knew it as their death day. However during the following weeks and months the forces widened and took control of the port at Cherbourg and the city of Caen. Then with a failed German counter offensive, the Allies were able to encircle 50,000 German soldiers in the Falaise pocket. To put more salt in the Germans gaping wound, the allies also invaded the southern side of France on August 15th during operation Dragoon. The push towards Paris was hard but a fast journey as subsequently by August 25th Paris was liberated.and the people rejoiced. The war still had several more months to go, but as far as the French knew, the 4 years of hell were finally over. Then on the 7th of May officially the war was over in Europe. Charles de Gaulle, was the leader of France after the end of World War Two due to his efforts to free France of its terror
