The United Kingdom

The Beginning Of The War

Leading up to the start of World War Two, The United Kingdom repeatedly warned Germany and its allies, or the Axis as they were known, to halt all of its advances on neighboring countries. However Neville Chamberlain’s lacks approach to Hitler and his regime, let him annex and take parts of several countries without serious reaction. Then in September of 1939 with the invasion of Poland, Neville had no choice but to declare war on Nazi Germany accumpanied by the UK’s ally France. Poland fell swiftly thereafter and the time of the Phoney War commenced, in which little to no conflict was made between either side. This lasted till May of 1940 when Germany invaded Denmark and Norway. Shortly after Germany successfully invaded the Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, and to the United Kingdom's Horror, France. France fell by the German strategy of moving through the arden forest in Luxembourg, catching the French forces completely off guard. With forces trapped in France, the British army was forced to go to the beaches in Dunkirk. Leaving as quickly as possible many British forces were unable to make it to Dunkirk on time and were subsequently killed or captured.

The Blitz

In 1940 right after the fall of France, Germany created a massive plan to invade England and take control of the only remaining enemy it faced. What commenced changed history forever. Germany repeatedly tried to gain aerial superiority over England however the RAF (Royal Air Force) denied the German air force from staying in Britain. These air battles were known as the Battle of Britain. There after seeing that Germany would have a very hard time trying to invade England directly , Germany decided to bomb London and several other cities throughout England. This event was known as The Blitz. The infrastructure of England was completely damaged for the rest of the war, greatly demoralizing the British people. At this time Germany flooded the Atlantic seas with their U-Boats, specialized attacking submarines designed to take out opposing convoys and cripple the Allies economy. By mid 1940 Great Britain started their North Africa Campaign, this being stated due to the declaration of war by Italy. These battles lasted for several years as Britain pushed from Egypt into Libya, then with the Italian counter offensive in late 1940 the British were beaten back. However in operation compass the British Commonwealth secured a massive blow to Italy with their 10th Arm being destroyed. This led to Germany deploying Erwin Rommel to North Africa , his nickname later became “The Desert Fox” because of his efficiency with his panzer divisions in the arid terrain.

The Rise in The East

By mid-1941 Germany had turned their focus away from Great Britain and towards the Soviet Union. Churchill saw this as a massive victory as it would now force Stalin, the leader of the Soviet Union, to join forces with the Allies and help rid Europe of the Nazi’s. In December of 1941 however the Royal navy in Singapore, a small port country in southeast Asia , took a massive blow by the Japanese Navy. This escalated into the later Attack on Pearl Harbor by the Japanese, ultimately bringing the United States into WW2. This combined with the Soviet Union also joining the War was exactly what Chirchill needed to keep his great country afloat.

The Push to Italy

By 1942 the British and American convoys in the Caribbean had taken massive hits by German U-Boats. Then in mid 1942 the Americans devised an idea of interlocked convoys which drastically reduced the casualties in manpower and supplies. By late 1942, Great Britain and its allies successfully planned an invasion of Western Africa named operation Torch. This being a bi-country assault into German controlled Morocco and Algeria with help from the Americans. This ultimately was a massive success, however both sides were inflicted with massive casualties. As General Montgomery made the Germans retreat into Tunisia, the battles from west Egypt and Libya resulted in a massive German encirclement in 1943. These victories then turned the Allies away from Africa and towards the Italian Peninsula. Through the past couple of years the British have attacked and destroyed several Italian ships through the Mediterranean. Then in July 1943 the Allies invaded the Italian island of Sicily. These subsequent battles were hard fought and the toughest the British have seen yet as they kicked the Axis out of Sicily in August of that year. Now just miles away from mainland Italy the Italian government led by Mussolini was leaning towards a collapse. By the mainland invasion in September the government had signed an armistice and moved to the south with the allies. Germany seeing their southern flank being exposed they moved a lot of their forces away from the Easern front against Stalin and instead to face the Allies in Italy.

The March To VE Day

By 1944 the Allies had invaded Italy to what was called the Winter Front, a line of Axis forces making the line to Rome nearly impossible because of its mountainous and fortified positions. This led to the Allies invading past the winter line in an amphibious assault. However this move was an ultimate failure as the beachhead did not push on the German forces quickly enough to merge with the main forces. Then with the Bolstering of several Allied forces they attacked past the Winter Line. In June of 1944 the Allies decided to invade the beaches of Normandy in Operation Overland. This ultimately was the largest Amphibious assault in human history. With the casualties reaching the 400,000’s the battles were stamped firmly into history. D-day as it is more commonly called, was a small step towards the ultimate liberation of France in August of that year and the push to take back the Netherlands, Belgium, and Luxembourg. Then as the Allied forces pushed into Germany in December of 1944, the Germans went on the offensive for the last time. The second battle of the Ardennes, or the Battle of the Bulge, ultimately took great casualties across the board and by February of 1945 the lines were roughly where they were in December of the previous year. This however crippled the German forces, and with the Soviets pushing towards Berlin already, on May 8th 1945 Germany fell as Berlin crumbled. In Europe the War was officially over, however in the Pacific the war still trudged on. However the Americans and the British had both been working on a special weapon, the Atomic Bomb. Theoretically capable of leveling cities. Then on September 2nd the war officially ended, after the Americans successfully dropped 2 nuclear bombs on August 6th and August 9th. The United Kingdom lost a total of roughly 450 thousand people. About 1 percent of the human population lost so that humanity and freedom could prevail.
