Japan

The Rising Tiger

The Japanese empire during World War One gained a large number of islands in the pacific from the Germans. Even though their efforts were minor they were able to sit on the winners side of the peace conference. This allowed them to enter into the League of Nations. The predecessor to the United Nations of modern day. The League of Nations main goal was to end global wars, and one of the large talking points of the United States’ Woodrow Wilson. However even though the league consisted of the largest nations of that time, their efforts to control war and terror were miniscule. Japan knew this and with their population rapidly expanding , their supplies rapidly depleting, and their space diminishing, sought to expand their borders. With their invasion of Manchuria in 1931 and China in 1937, the league became more and more furious with this rising empire in the east. Other than verbal warnings however the league made little to no physical reparations for Japan. The Japanese invasion of China started on July 7th 1937 with the Marco Polo incident, where Japanese and Chinese forces got into a dispute which escalated into an invasion by Japan. China was supported by The United States and the Soviet Union, this raised tensions between them and Japan. The Japanese Chinese war or Sino-Japanese war, constituted the majority of the casualties of the pacific theater in World War Two. With upwards of 25 Million Chinese civilians dying to the imperial Japanese. The numerous atrocities committed by the Japanese were unspeakable, some call this the “Pacific holocaust”, comparing it to the mass genocide by the Germans during the same time. Then on the 22nd of september 1940 the Japanese invaded French IndoChina and officially joined world war two by signing the Tripartite Pact with Germany and Italy. The war with China however was taking a massive toll on Japanese resources. Their largest supplier of Oil was the United States, this was till America placed an embargo on Japan demanding their withdrawal from China and peace with the rest of Asia. The emperor saw this as a massive offense. The people of Japan saw themselves as higher beings than the rest of the world. They saw it as their right to conquer the rest of Asia. In their mind to have the weak country of The United States make demands like this was unforgivable. War proportions commenced.

The Might of The Japanese

The Allies encompassing all of The United Kingdom's colonies, followed suit of the United states and embargoed Japan. Japan had to act fast or could possibly deplete all their resources and have to retreat from the war. Plans had been made and on December 7th, a day which will live in infamy, Japan simultaneously invaded Pearl Harbor, Wake Island, Thailand, Guam, Malaya, Hong Kong, and The Philippine islands. During the attack on Pearl harbor, a naval port of The United States, the Japanese destroyed several American Battleships and virtually wiped the port of all airbases and air capabilities. The United States had stationed almost all of their Battleships here because of the defense the shallow water provided. It was deep enough that the ships could dock in the harbor, but too shallow for a Torpedo. The Japanese subverted this by installing wooden planks to their torpedoes. This allowed them to float right under the water. This act forced the United States to declare war on Japan. Japan's main goal in this attack was to cripple the United states for long enough that they could establish their pacific empire. However to Japan’s horror, a large chunk of American ships were not present such as their Aircraft Carriers. A staple flag ship of the modern era, eventually being seen as far more capable than a BattleShip. This coupled with the power of the United States people, seeing the Pearl Harbor Attacks as barbaric. The unification of the American people to bolster their Industry was eventually Japan's worst enemy. By 1942, the Japanese Empire had launched offensives in New Guinea, Singapore, Burma, Yunnan and India, the Solomons, Timor, Aleutian Islands, Christmas Island and the Andaman Islands. Around this time Japan had cultivated a liking towards biological weapons, with a horrifying display during the air raid of Ningbo where Japan dropped plagued flees over the populace. Japan consistently has been trying to expand what they describe as the Asian Co-Prosperity Sphere. A league of Asian countries unified under the alignment of Asians, however as many countries have seen the atrocities committed by the Japanese the members started to become entirely Japanese puppet states. Hong Kong finally fell on December 25th with Malaya following suit shortly after, this was aided by the sinking of the British ship HMS Prince of Whales. Then on February 25th Singapore followed suit with the largest surrender of British troops in history, 80,000 men were captured and forced into Japanese internment camps coupled with the 50,000 also captured in malaya. Japan then captured the oil fields in the Dutch East Indies, a massive victory for Japan as this allowed them to supply more and more of their naval fleet.

The Turing Point

By 1942, Japan saw the only way to win this war was to use the victory at Pearl Harbor to their utmost advantage. They saw this as eliminating the American fleet, then in May of that year, The United States enacted the Doolittle Raid. A bombing of the Japanese Capital of Tokyo, this enraged the Emperor and made the Japanese people fearful of America. In May, the battle of the Coral Sea commenced, where Japan attempted to take port Moresby in New Guinea. This was a revolutionary battle as it was the first to be fought entirely by Aircraft carriers. This battle was a tactical victory for Japan as they sank prominent American ships, however it was a strategic victory for The United States as they prevented Japan from taking the port. The first time since the beginning of the war that Japan was forced to retreat from opposing forces. This paved the way for one of the most influential naval battles in history. The battle of Midway was on the 4th of June 1942. The Japanese Navy sought to take Midway Island, by subverting the United States Navy away with false radio callings. However American radio analysts noticed their plan, catching the Japanese by surprise. The United States sent their fleet of Aircraft Carriers to meet the Japanese Navy. What commenced was spectacular, for the United States anyways, horrifying for the Japanese. Previously in the war the Japanese Zero airplane rained supreme over the pacific, however now with the American Hellcat and eventual mustang, America gave Japan a tough fight in the air. In the battle of midway a total of 4 aircraft carriers were destroyed, completely crippling the Japanese Navy. This is marked as the major turning point for the war in the Pacific.

Japan’s Ultimate End

Australian land forces defeated Japanese Marines in New Guinea at the Battle of Milne Bay in September 1942, which was the first land defeat suffered by the Japanese in the Pacific. Further victories by the Allies at Guadalcanal in September 1942, and New Guinea in 1943 put the Empire of Japan on the defensive for the remainder of the war. The Battle of Guadalcanal was quite taxing for Japan as their Oil capacities started to waver. In the subsequent couple of years The United States took island after island in the pacific. The Japanese stood their ground till the last man, fighting viciously. In their code of conduct surrendering was a sign of ultimate weakness, punishable by death. Honor was seen as more valuable than life in self. This is why during the war you saw actions such as Kamikaze attacks on ships, and Banzai attacks by the infantry. Kamikaze attacks were where pilots saw it as their duty to die for their country, in an action of complete honor they would fly their plane into enemy ships causing immense damage to the hull. A Banzai charge was one where a horde of Japanese soldiers would charge at on coming Americans, this sounds like it would be effective however the Americans would simply mow the Japanese down with their machine guns as they approached. The Japanese Military didn’t take human life into consideration, the Japanese lost upwards of 3 Million men during World war 2. By late 1944 the United States had launched reclamation efforts in the Philippines and started their advance towards mainland Japan starting with the famous Battle of Iwo Jima in February of 1945. Where the United States hit stiff resistance from Japan's Fortified positions in the mountains of the island. Here the American marines saw some of the hardest fighting to date. Japan let no ground be taken without a fight. Shortly after in April of 1945 America attacked the island of Okinawa. Seen as Japan’s last stand against the Americans, Japan fought tooth and nail. Over a hundred thousand men died in the months it took for Japan to concede the island. Being the deadliest battle of the pacific theater, to say Japan put up a fight is an understatement. The Americans tried to have the Japanese surrender on multiple occasions. However the emperor did not want to surrender and refute his honor as the leader of the empire. This decision was made infinitely more difficult as the United states dropped the first atomic bomb on foreign soil with the nuclear bombing of Hiroshima on August 6th 1945. Even though over a hundred thousand civilians were obliterated in this bombing, Emperor HiroHito refused to sign a peace treaty.This forced the United States to drop the second atomic bomb on August 9th 1945 over Nagasaki, killing another 75,000 civilians. This happened on the same day as the Soviet invasion and capture of Manchuria. It's hard to tell which the Emperor surrendered because of but by September 2nd 1945 the war was officially over with the Emperor's official surrender.

After the War

Though the war was over Japan was not done fighting the Americans. Many military leaders and soldiers were convicted of war crimes proceeding the war. Along with this the Allies forced Japan to surrender all captured territories, along with surrendering the Korean peninsula. This came with the Allies completely redoing the Japanese government including rewriting the constitution. The United States, and some corresponding allies, were used as Japan's military force. Whereas Japan had defensive military power, it was limited greatly by the constitution. Not until 2015 did bills begin to be formed moving towards a more militarily stable Japanese state.
