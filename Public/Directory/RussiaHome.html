Soviet Union

The Rise of Stalin

On January 21st 1924, following the death of Vladimir Lennon, Joseph Stalin took power over the Soviet Union. Lennon before his death instructed his cabinet that under no circumstances the crazed megalomaniac Joseph Stalin should take power, however Stalin empowered the people and became so famous it would be a travesty for him not to become Leader. Hindsight is 20/20 as we can now see, A travesty would be an understatement for him coming into power. While actually in power Stalin took his broken country into industrial super power. This however was built off the starvation and death of millions of his own people. Through the Five year plan, citizens were forced to work in horrible conditions while watching themselves and their families die of hunger. In 1936 - 1938 Stalin would enact the great purge of the officers, scientists, politicians, doctors, and really anyone he demanded to be killed. This was his way of establishing ultimate control over his people, and ultimate control over power. Then in August of 1939 Joseph Stalin came to an aliance with Adolf Hitler of Nazi Germany. This eventually paved the way for the Joint invasion of Poland that september. The Nazis invaded and officially stated World War 2 by invading poland on September 1st. Then on September 17th the Soviet Union followed suit and invaded Poland from the East side. This successfully sandwiched Poland into submission on the 27th of the same month. Poland was split between Germany and Poland. Officially making an open border between Germany and the Soviet Union. This eventually led to the annexation of Lithuania , Estonia, Moldova, and Latvia into the Soviet Union.

Winter War

Then on November 30th 1939, the Soviet Union invaded Finland in the Winter War. The Soviets however were unable to invade very far into Finland as their defenses were too strong in the northern winter. The Soviets instead annexed a small portion of Finland instead. This was seen as a massive embarrassment. As the Soviets still continued to grow their Industrial power by 1940 the Axis powers were officially formed between Germany , Italy, and Japan. Stalin saw this as an opportunity to make his alliance with Hitler more official by having the Soviet Union join the Axis. This however was seen as a terrible idea from his correspondence, but more importantly Germany likely would have never agreed to let them join as Hitler had different Ideas for his massive soviet neighbor.

The Beginning

On the 22nd of June 1941 Germany enacted operation Barbarossa, the invasion of the Soviet Union. After Germany’s failed attempt to take control of England, Germany saw Russia as their best next bet. Germany pushed quickly into the under defended Soviet border. Stalin was more than shocked he was in utter disbelief. He said to every correspondent, telling him about the German Invasion, that they were definitely mistaken. This led to a dilaid military reaction and allowed the Germans to get increasingly closer to Moscow. In the days after the initial Invasion Stalin isolated himself from board meetings, until he eventually emerged and took the title Marshal Of The Soviet Union. This was the highest military title in the Soviet Union. In the first 3 weeks in the war The Soviets lost over 750 thousand men and hundreds of thousands more were captured. The Soviets desperately tried to defend from the German onslaught. This was brought on by the Soviet doctrine of war to defend then go on minor offenses, however with the might of Germany on its border this plan definitely did not work. This cultivated into the first battle of Kiev where the Soviets lost over 650,000 men in battle. The generals could see that this plan based on the original Red army would not work. By the end of 1941 they had lost a terrifying 4 million men with another 3 million being captured and sent to concentration camps where most would later die.

The Turning Point

The Germans posted a large force to capture the port city of Leningrad. This resulted in Stalin sending 1 million soldiers to stay posted and garrison the city. What resulted was Germany sieging the city and starving the populous. Subsequently Stalin made agreements with its original counterpart the United Kingdom and its allies, to sign a peace agreement. Stalin was sure of the fact that as long as the Allies supplied his efforts Germany would not be able to take The Soviet Union. Stalin's arrogance was both a blessing and a curse, as the Germans got within 30 kilometers of Moscow. In 1942 As Stalin made sure the soviets stood their ground he enacted the first major Soviet offensive. Effectively pushing the Germans away from Moscow, the first major victory for the Soviets. The Soviets became a little arrogant themselves as they took the city of Kharkov they inevitably got themselves completely encircled with 200,000 men being killed or captured. This coupled with the failing defenses of the southern section of Russia. Germany trudged through trying to take the strategic area of the Crimea. During a meeting with the British prime minister Winston Churchill Stalin discussed ways to divert German forces from his border. Ultimately the conclusion was set for the British and Americans to invade northern Africa. Which they did later that year. The Germans, assuming the Russians were on the brink of surrendering, advanced again into the south, trying to encircle the Soviets in Stalingrad and advance to the Caspian Sea. As the Germans trudged to Stalingrad, they encountered ultimately the deadliest battle in human history. Even though the Soviets lost almost 2 million soldiers in this one battle, they successfully killed roughly 500,000 Germans and encircled 250,000 more. This was a major turning point for the soviets.

Soviet industry

By mid-1943 Germany had started to go on the retreat. The Soviet industry back in 1941 in some parts of the country had to up and move hundreds of miles away before the Germans could capture and destroy them. This eventually led to the massive industrial advantage the soviets had over Germany. By this time the Soviets could produce 4X the amount of tanks and ammunition as their German counterparts, this had to do with Soviet design. The T-34 were designed with sloped armor allowing them to have a lot less metal and have the same if not better defenses. Whereas German tanks were stronger, they were vastly harder to make and required a lot more materials. In just this right, Germans got surrounded by the sheer volume of soviet tanks. To couple Soviet generals enforced strict policies of no retreats and hard discipline in combat.

The End of Hell

The Soviets after Stalingrad took a defensive position to regroup after the massive casualties they endured. Then took on fighting back against the Germans slowly putting them on the run. The Soviets by the end of 1943 took back a significant portion of the territory it lost. By the beginning of 1944 the Soviets were able to finally resupply Leningrad from the 3 year long siege. 600,000 to 2 Million civilians died in this siege the ones who were able to survive did so by maintaining the food factories and by traitorously escaping. Then later that year the Soviets met with the Allies again and Stalin enforced the need to push into Germany’s western flank on the French coast. Like with the idea of invading north Africa, the Allies pursued this idea later that year with operation Overlord. With the Germans in a duel front war, they had no choice but to take significant forces away from its eastern flank. This only sped up the soviets advancement. In Operation Bagration the Soviets made one of their best offenses in the war. Pushing through Belarus further towards central Germany. Later that year the Soviets recaptured Lithuania and Estonia only losing 200,000 men in the process. Then in the Budapest Offensive the Soviets pushed to capture hungry. This however ended in a Soviet failure, Stalin refusing to even acknowledge its existence. Then in 1945 the soviets were on offenses all over the border. Capturing countries and territory previously being controlled by the Nazis. Though the Soviets were allied with the Allies, the atrocities committed by their red Army can not be overlooked. Tens of thousands of German citizens were raped an murdered as the soviets plundered the German territories. Thousands of war crimes were committed by the soviets in the later years of the war. This being brought on by the fact the Germans would do almost the same thing as they were entering russia. The russian philosophy of an eye for an eye ran true for this day and age. The Russians marched further and further into Germany directed towards Berlin. Until on the 16th of April 1945 they successfully surrounded Berlin. They then proceeded to artillery shell the city to absolute rubble. On the 30th of the same month Adolf Hitler and his Wife Eva Braun committed suicide in a bunker in Berlin. Then on May 8th Germany surrendered to the Allies. Ending the brutal war in Europe. However the Japanese were still rampaging the Pacific. On the 9th of August 1945 Russia invaded Japanese controlled Manchuria. They quickly took it over and being the same day as the nuclear bombing of Nagasaki, Japan officially surrendered later that month. Then capitulated on september 2nd. Officially ending World War 2.
