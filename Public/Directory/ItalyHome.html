Italy

The Rise and Fall of Mussolini

Italy was a part of the Allies during World War One, the reason they joined in the first place was based on promises of major territorial gains after they won. However after the treaty of Versailles in 1918 was signed, the territory they gained was very minimal compared to what they were promised. This infuriated the Italian populace, seeing how their economy took a major hit during the war while several thousands died fighting it. During this time Bunito Mussolini had returned from the war injured and started to lead the pro Fascist party rising in Italy. In 1920 Mussolini and his Black Shirt Fascist group started attacking political groups, and by 1921 held significant control over the majority in Italy. In 1922 Mussolini by order of the King was ordained the Prime Minister. In 1935 Italy invaded Ethiopia conquering the rest of the country, this raised tensions between Italy and the current Allies (The UK and France). Later that decade Italy and Nazi Germany became fairly close, seeing how they were the only two major fascist countries in Europe. However Adolf Hitler the leader of Germany despised Mussolini, and the lack of trust was very eminent. By 1939 Germany had invaded Poland, officially starting world war 2. Italy stayed out of the war entirely until in June of 1940 when Germany invaded and conquered France, Italy then declared war on the Allies thinking the war was ultimately over. The United Kingdom ultimately was a larger threat than anticipated. As several attacks were made on Italy’s north african positions by the British Egyptians. The real offensive by the Italians began in October as they invaded Greece through Albania. This however was an ultimate disaster for the Italians, and in 1941 had to be rescued by the Germans. In constant battle with the British in Libya and in the Mediterranean, Italy stood no chance. By 1943 At the decisive second battle of El-Alamein in Egypt Italy eventually lost all positions in Africa.

Italian Collapse

Italy’s failure through the war came from several factors. The Major three were as follows, Low Moral, Low Supplies, and Poor General Leadership. The Soldiers were fighting very far from home, in hot desolate areas, for reasons barely anyone believed in. The drive to win battles had no meaning. With no supplies to be given to the soldiers, even if the men wanted to be there they couldn’t sustain the fight. The allies constantly bombed italian industry so even when they were supplied with raw materials the low amount of factories caused Italian supply shortages to sky rocket. This coupled with Italy’s incapability to transfer resources across the Mediterranean, because of the British Navy,made Italian forces in Africa suffer extreme attrition. Italian command made any effort by the Italians utterly pointless, as the Generals pointed the front line troops into impossible positions. Now with the Allies creeping up towards mainland Italy, the Italian government started taking massive flack from the people. With communist uprisings and democratic strikes, Italy fell into political and economic turmoil. Bombing of major cities tore more and more supplies away from the populous causing a pandemic of food shortages. By July 1943 the Allies had invaded and shortly thereafter captured the large island of sicily. After this major disaster Italian government officials cast out Mussolini and installed marshal Pietro Badoglio. After which political demonstrations were made where statues of Mussolini were torn down along with and fascist flags or symbols. Then after a massacre of civilians by the italian troops, A Italian mob stormed Rome capturing and imprisoning Mussolini. While also stomping out the entirety of the Fascist party. Badoglio then started a temporary government, assuring the people the war with the Allies would still continue. However in september of 1943 Italy signed an armistice with the Allies. Instantly the Germans stormed through Italy capturing Rome and meeting the Allies in the south. Extremely tough fighting commenced, but by June 1944 the Allies had re-captured Rome and started their push towards Germany. Prior to German explosion of Italy, Mussolini was taken out of prison by the Germans and had him become in control of a Italian fascist regime. Mussolini had little power over the Italian people and by April of 1945 Mussolini saw defeat on the horizon and tried partitioning the allies to make a deal with him. However when that ultimately failed, Mussolini tried to escape to Switzerland, where he was found and shot. Though Italy’s efforts in the war were futile, the eventual peace with the allies helped divert German forces away from the main fronts and sped the defeat of the Germans greatly. The war eventually ended in Europe on May 8th of 1945.
